Handling Mental Health

handling mental health

How can I help Others?

  1. Listen
  2. Offer reassurance
  3. Stay calm
  4. Be patient
  5. Try not to make assumptions
  6. Keep social contact

How can I help Myself?

  1. Value yourself
  2. Take care of your body
  3. Surround yourself with good people
  4. Set realistic goals
  5. Learn how to deal with stress
  6. Get help when you need it

Having good mental health does not imply you will never have terrible moments or face emotional problems. We all experience disappointment, loss, and change. Even while they are typical aspects of life, they can create despair, anxiety, and tension. However, just as physically healthy individuals recover faster from disease or accident, those with excellent mental health recover faster from hardship, trauma, and stress. This skill is known as resilience.

When a friend, family member, or someone we care about is going through a difficult moment, it can be difficult to grasp what they are going through or how we might support them. However, for the majority of individuals, simply asking whether they are okay and listening may make a big impact. It's frequently a huge relief for them to know that you're aware that they're going through a difficult moment and that you're eager to listen and aid if necessary.
