What is Mental Health Awareness?

What is the importance of Mental Health Awareness?

Who can benefit from Mental Health Awareness?

Mental Health Awareness was established to educate the public about mental illness, raise public awareness about research and treatments, decrease the stigma associated with mental illness, and commemorate mental illness healing. People frequently suffer in silence and may not seek treatment for their problems due to myths and stigma around mental health difficulties. Mental health awareness is a critical project for increasing understanding of mental health disorders and increasing access to healthcare for those in need.

Whether or not a person has a mental health problem, Mental Health Awareness helps people acknowledge how mental illness affects their life, informs them about available resources, and emphasizes methods to advocate. Recognition and education are major elements in removing the stigma that surrounds mental illness. Mental Health Awarenessprovides an opportunity to start a communication. Talking honestly about mental health may help to dispel myths and stigma, as well as urge individuals in need to get treatment and establish a support network.

Mental Health Awareness helps those with a condition overcome the stigma and get access to treatment. It helps their friends, family, and loved ones better understand their condition and connect with support networks. It helps the providers who treat those with a condition receive funding for their research and treatment so they can better care for all our mental health. In short, we can all benefit from Mental Health Awareness.

What Mental Health Disorders Need More Awareness

what mental health disorders need more awareness

Bipolar Disorder:

Also known as manic-depressive disease or manic depression, bipolar disorder is classified into three categories. This disorder produces unpleasant mood, energy, and focus swings, as well as disruptions in everyday tasks. They are classified into several kinds.

Read more about Bipolar Disorder

Anxiety:

They are classified into several kinds. They all start with the same issue: overpowering dread and worry that dictates your existence. Anxiety is so severe that it interferes with job, relationships, education, and life in general. Panic disorder, phobias, and social phobias are all members of the anxiety disorder family.

Read more about Anxiety

Substance Use Disorder:

These disorders are characterized by the repeated and abusive use of alcohol and drugs. They are frequently used to self-medicate symptoms of mental illness. In fact, over half of those who have a mental condition also have a drug or alcohol addiction. A type of mood disorder.

Read more about Substance Use Disorder

Depression:

A type of mood disorder. People frequently misdiagnose it as the blues or a foul mood. The symptoms can become so severe that they interfere with daily living and the capacity to do routine tasks. Also called major depressive disorder or clinical depression, it affects how you feel, think and behave and can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems.

Read more about Depression

Schizophrenia:

A severe mental condition that has an impact on how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. People suffering with schizophrenia may appear to have lost contact with reality, which can be upsetting for them as well as their family and friends. Schizophrenia symptoms can make it difficult to engage in normal, everyday activities, but effective treatments are available. Many persons who undergo therapy are able to attend school or work, gain independence, and maintain personal connections

Read more about Schizophrenia

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder

Shell shock, also known as combat fatigue syndrome, is a dangerous disorder that can occur after a person has undergone or witnessed a traumatic or frightening incident involving major bodily damage or threat. PTSD is a long-term effect of traumatic experiences that evoke great fear, helplessness, or terror.

Read more about PTSD

Suicidal Ideation:

Suicidal thoughts or ideas are a wide phrase used to represent a variety of thoughts, wishes, and preoccupations with death and suicide. Because there is no globally acknowledged uniform definition of SI, doctors, researchers, and educators face continual issues. In research projects, for example, SI is commonly given distinct operational meanings. This impairs the capacity to compare data across research and is commonly cited as a drawback in meta-analyses of suicidality. Suicide planning deliberations are included in some SI definitions, while others consider planning to be a distinct stage.
